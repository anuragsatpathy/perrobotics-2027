Robots as Pervasive Computing Agents

Robots are becoming embodied components of smart environments. PerRobotics 2027 will bring together researchers working across pervasive computing, robotics, sensing, IoT, edge intelligence, mobile and distributed systems, cyber-physical systems, and human-computer interaction.

Sense

Context-aware robotic perception using mobile, wearable, environmental, and infrastructure-based sensors.

Think

Real-time edge, fog, cloud, and foundation-model intelligence for resource-constrained robotic systems.

Coordinate

Safe human-robot and multi-robot coordination across dynamic, connected, and human-centered environments.

Call for Papers

We invite original research, system designs, experimental studies, experience reports, demonstrations, and forward-looking position papers at the intersection of robotics and pervasive computing.

  • Context-aware perception and sensing for robots in smart environments
  • Integration of robots with mobile, wearable, and infrastructure-based IoT sensors
  • Edge, fog, and cloud-assisted robotic intelligence
  • Visual grounding for robotic tasks
  • Real-time and resource-constrained inference for robotic systems
  • Distributed intelligence, task offloading, and latency-aware robot control
  • Multi-robot coordination in pervasive environments
  • Human-in-the-loop and human-centered robotic systems
  • Multimodal human-robot interaction through speech, gestures, and wearable sensing
  • Privacy, trust, safety, and security in pervasive robotic systems
  • Energy-aware and resource-aware robot operation
  • Digital twins, simulation, and testbeds for pervasive robotics
  • Robotic path planning
  • Autonomous ground and air mobility
  • Embodied AI and embodied intelligence in pervasive environments
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) systems and foundation models for robotics
  • Foundation models, LLMs, and vision-language models for robotic perception and decision-making
  • Context-aware task planning and long-horizon autonomous decision making
  • Intermittent computing in robotics
  • Pervasive robotics for healthcare, eldercare, logistics, transportation, smart campuses, agriculture, emergency response, and public safety

Submission Guidelines

All submissions and reviews will go through the official PerCom conference management system, HotCRP. The workshop track link will be posted here when available.

Paper Requirements

  • Workshop papers follow the standard PerCom workshop length limit: 6 pages.
  • Authors may include 1 additional page, for a maximum of 7 pages total.
  • All accepted papers must be presented in person by a designated presenter, preferably one of the authors.
  • Proxy or remote presentations are not allowed unless pre-approved by the workshop chairs, general chairs, and steering committee at least one week in advance.

Registration Policy

  • PerCom does not have a workshop-only registration fee.
  • Each accepted paper requires full PerCom registration.
  • There is no student-rate registration for accepted workshop papers.
  • Papers without a valid full registration or that are not presented in-person will be excluded from the proceedings.
